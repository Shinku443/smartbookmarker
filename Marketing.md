# Emperor Marketing Plan

A clear, staged strategy for introducing Emperor Library to real users and growing it into a polished, premium bookmarking and knowledge‑management product.

---

## 1. Positioning

**Core pitch:**

> Emperor is a fast, local‑first, deeply customizable knowledge library that turns your bookmarks into a living system — not a graveyard.

**Key differentiators:**
- Local‑first performance and instant UI
- Deep structure (books, nested books, smart collections)
- Strong tagging and multi‑select workflows
- Future AI enhancements as first‑class features
- Clean, modern, distraction‑free design

---

## 2. Stage 1 — Private Builder Mode

**Goal:** Make Emperor “real” enough for 10–20 power users.

### Deliverables
- Simple landing page with:
  - Product overview
  - Screenshots
  - Email waitlist
- Lightweight devlog:
  - Short posts per release
  - GIFs of new features
- Early community presence:
  - Twitter/X (dev audience)
  - Indie Hackers
  - Relevant subreddits (tasteful, non‑spammy)

### Success metrics
- 20–50 waitlist signups
- 5–10 active early testers
- Qualitative feedback on UX and workflows

---

## 3. Stage 2 — Closed Beta

**Goal:** Validate workflows with real users who care about bookmarks and knowledge organization.

### Actions
- Invite early testers:
  - Developers
  - Designers
  - Researchers
  - Indie hackers
- Encourage importing existing bookmarks
- Provide onboarding:
  - Short guide
  - Example workflows
- Collect feedback:
  - In‑app prompts
  - Occasional 1:1 calls

### Success metrics
- 100+ imported bookmarks per tester
- Clear patterns in requested features
- Stable retention over 2–3 weeks

---

## 4. Stage 3 — Public Beta

**Goal:** Position Emperor as a serious alternative to Raindrop/Notion bookmarks.

### Launch channels
- Product Hunt
- Hacker News (Show HN)
- Twitter/X launch thread
- Devlog announcement

### Offering
- Free tier:
  - Local‑only
  - Limited sync (optional)
- Paid tier:
  - Full sync
  - AI metadata
  - Smart collections
  - Priority support

### Content to publish
- “How I built Emperor Library”
- “From bookmarks to knowledge library”
- “Why Raindrop and Notion aren’t enough for power users”

### Success metrics
- 500–1,000 signups
- 10–20 paying users
- Strong feedback loop

---

## 5. Stage 4 — Focused Growth

**Goal:** Grow a passionate niche before going broad.

### Target audiences
- Developers
- Designers
- Researchers
- Indie hackers

### Content strategy
- Workflow demos:
  - “How I organize design inspiration”
  - “My research workflow with Emperor”
  - “Replacing Raindrop + Notion bookmarks”
- YouTube demos
- Blog tutorials
- Twitter/X threads

### Success metrics
- Organic growth
- Community contributions
- Word‑of‑mouth adoption

---

## 6. Stage 5 — Long‑Term Expansion

**Goal:** Build a sustainable product ecosystem.

### Future opportunities
- Browser extension
- Mobile app / PWA
- Collaboration features
- AI‑powered summaries and digests
- Enterprise knowledge library
